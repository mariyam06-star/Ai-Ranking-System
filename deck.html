🎯 The Problem

Why Keyword Filters Fail

Keyword matching finds people who used the right words — not people who can do the job.

❌
Keyword Matching

  • Misses "bi-encoder" = "Siamese network"
  • Misses "LTR" = "Learning to Rank"
  • Rewards keyword stuffing in summaries
  • Ignores proficiency depth & duration
  • Can't detect "Accountant who took an online RAG course"
  • Misses honeypots with impossible timelines

✅
Our Approach

  • 50+ weighted skill signals with proficiency depth
  • Duration-scaled experience boost per skill
  • Career history proves shipped systems, not just listed skills
  • Behavioral signals (activity, notice, GitHub, RRR)
  • Hard disqualifiers for non-technical histories
  • Honeypot detection removes fraudulent profiles first

🍯 Honeypot Detection

Removing Fraudulent Profiles

94 profiles removed — two distinct traps identified through exploratory data analysis.

🕐

Trap 1 — Impossible Timeline

73 candidates claim to have worked at Sarvam AI or Krutrim before these companies were founded in late 2023.

# Detection rule if company in {"Sarvam AI", "Krutrim"}: if start_year < 2023: mark_as_honeypot()
⚡

Trap 2 — Instant Expert

21 candidates list skills with proficiency = "expert" but duration_months = 0 — claiming mastery with zero practice.

# Detection rule for skill in candidate.skills: if skill.proficiency == "expert": if skill.duration_months == 0: mark_as_honeypot()
